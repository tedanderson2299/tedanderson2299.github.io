About Me

View Resume

I am a software product manager and strategist for DataArt Solutions in New York City. I help craft product vision, conduct UX research, and analyze technology. I am especially proud of contributing to the design and development of groundbreaking software for the 988-Suicide Hotline.

Before DataArt, I studied geosciences and played lacrosse at Williams College. As a geoscientist, my passions for the Earth evolved to include technology and system design, as I designed programs to model complex earth systems such as Arctic Sea ice or the atmosphere. As my love for technology grew, so did my love for building, leading me to experiment with several start-ups ranging from recycling machines to fin-techs.

At home I tinker with building mobile applications, read and write about US history, trail run, and throw epic dinner parties.

Geospatial Analysis & Webmaps

Ubranization Land Classification Analysis

Bozeman, Montanna grew from 37,000 people in 2010 to 53,000 in 2020. This map explores urbanization in Bozeman. The default layers and slider allow you to visualize 2009 and 2022 True Color imagery. The layer control at the top right allows you to view land a classification analysis of the 2009 and 2022 imagery made using Google Earth Engine IDE and supervised classification and regression tree analysis.


Long train Rides to Lacrosse

I love lacrosse so much I am willing to travel long distances to play. Check out my weekly route to men's league in Boston. I made this map by querying publicly available transit data and mapping my routes using LeafletJS an open source webmapping library.


Landsat 5 NDVI Analysis

This map uses NDVI indexing to highlight the effect of water infrastructure failure after hurricane Katrina in New Orleans in 2005. Landsat 8 with band combinations 5, 4, 3.
